How does COVID-19 spread?

The virus is transmitted through direct contact with respiratory droplets of an infected person.

Personal Contact


Direct skin to skin contact increase likelihood of infection. Whether or not they have symptoms, infected people can be contagious and the virus can spread from them to other people.

Gatherings


Gatherings may bring a massive infection to all people that attend. Avoid crowded places, poorly ventilated, indoor locations and avoid prolonged contact with others. Spend more time outdoors than indoors

Touching Infected Surface


Touching infected surface may cause you to get the virus. Avoid touching surfaces, especially in public settings or health facilities, in case people infected with COVID-19 have touched them. Clean surfaces regularly with standard disinfectants.

Distance


Not keeping the 2 meter distance will cause the virus to be easily transmitted to one person to another.

How can I avoid being infected by COVID-19?

  • Maintain a safe distance from others (at least 2 meter), even if they don’t appear to be sick.
  • Wear a mask in public, especially indoors or when physical distancing is not possible.
  • Choose open, well-ventilated spaces over closed ones. Open a window if indoors.
  • Clean your hands often. Use soap and water, or an alcohol-based hand rub.
  • Get vaccinated when it’s your turn. Follow local guidance about vaccination.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with your bent elbow or a tissue when you cough or sneeze.
  • Stay home if you feel unwell.
